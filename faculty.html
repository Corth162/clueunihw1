Colonel Mustard

With a background in military-grade computing, Colonel Mustard is the university’s expert in computer engineering and embedded systems. His booming voice commands attention, and his hands-on labs push students to build and optimize hardware for high-performance applications. He’s a stickler for efficiency, often comparing debugging hardware to battlefield tactics. His students quickly learn that he expects nothing less than precision and discipline in their work.

Mr. Green

With a background in finance and a penchant for numbers, Mr. Green teaches data science and algorithmic trading, showing students how to harness data for real-world decision-making. He’s a master of statistical modeling, machine learning applications in finance, and predictive analytics. While his polished exterior suggests a life of luxury, he’s passionate about ensuring students grasp the ethical implications of data use.

Miss Scarlett

Miss Scarlett is as sharp as she is stylish, known for her expertise in cybersecurity and ethical hacking. She teaches her students how to think like a hacker to defend against cyber threats, often demonstrating live penetration tests in class. Rumors swirl that she once worked as a white-hat hacker for a top-secret government agency, but she never confirms nor denies them. Her classes are intense, hands-on, and known for their unpredictability—much like the professor herself..

Professor Plum

A brilliant but eccentric mind, Professor Plum is a leading researcher in AI and machine learning, specializing in neural networks and deep learning. Known for his absent-minded nature, he often loses track of time while training models, yet his breakthroughs in algorithm optimization have revolutionized the field. His students admire his ability to make complex theories digestible—though they also keep an eye on his ever-growing collection of unfinished projects.

Mrs. Peacock

Elegance meets innovation in Mrs. Peacock, the university’s leading expert on human-computer interaction and user experience design. She emphasizes the importance of intuitive interfaces and accessibility, teaching students how to craft technology that seamlessly integrates into users’ lives. With a keen eye for aesthetics and usability, she believes that no program is complete until it is both functional and beautiful. Her classes often involve interactive projects, ensuring students leave with practical, portfolio-ready designs.

Mrs. White

Efficient, pragmatic, and unwaveringly detail-oriented, Mrs. White is the backbone of the software engineering department. She instills in her students the importance of clean, maintainable code, software testing, and agile development. Known for her no-nonsense approach, she runs her classroom like a well-oiled machine, ensuring that students graduate with the discipline and problem-solving skills necessary for large-scale software development. “A messy codebase,” she often warns, “is just as bad as a messy kitchen.”
